GANESHA STATUE

Location :

Ganesha statue is located inside the left chamber of Shiva temple. On the left side of Shiva temple, go upstairs. There is one chamber over there, go inside.

Descriptions :

  • Ganesha is a symbol of knowledge in Hindu beliefs.

  • It has elephant-formed head, with large ears, long nose, and big belly.

  • Historically, Ganesha is long believed by people as God of knowledge. Large ears mean that people should listen to the virtue for gain many insights.

  • Its big belly has meaning that not only listen to the virtue but also remember it and save it inside the belly, so the belly is full of knowledge.

  • In the story in Hindu mythology, Ganesha was the son of Shiva and he is the one that inherits shiva intelligence for the human beings.

  • Ganesha has role to give human intelligence more than any other creature in the world.
